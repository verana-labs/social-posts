# Verana's mission: a public trust layer with a built-in separation of powers

## Platform

## Content

### LinkedIn

Rebuilding trust isn't just about technology. It's about who holds the power.

Verana is rebuilding digital trust for an era of AI agents, identity theft, and opaque online governance. But a trust layer for the internet can't be owned or controlled by any single party. So Verana is built on a deliberate separation of powers, carried by two independent institutions.

🟣 The Verana Foundation stewards the shared, off-network assets. It owns the open specifications, hosts and maintains the open-source software (Apache 2.0), and drives adoption. It cannot govern, secure, or operate the live network.

🔵 The Verana Council governs and secures the live network. Its members each run a validator node, and together they author the governance frameworks. Membership is free, open to enterprises, governments, and standards bodies. It cannot own the specifications or the token.

Two bodies, fully separate by design. Neither can capture the other. That is how you build a public trust layer that stays public.

Foundation membership and Council seats are open now.
