## Content

### LinkedIn

**Agent Identity isn't an AI problem. It's a trust problem.**

Today's internet runs on "connect first, verify later." You reach a service through an opaque URL and *hope* it's who it claims to be. For AI agents acting on our behalf, that's untenable — but the truth is it was never really safe for any of us.

Verifiable Trust inverts it: **verify first, then connect.**

Every service — an API, a website, or an autonomous AI agent — is identified not by a URL, but by a **resolvable DID**. Before the first interaction, it presents Verifiable Credentials proving *who operates it, what it's for, and that it's authorized.* Your agent resolves those credentials, checks them against a public registry, and shows you a **Proof-of-Trust** — all before a single byte of real interaction is exchanged.

Agent Identity is just identity, done right. It works for AI agents because it works for everything you connect to.

"Verify first, then connect" — applied uniformly to humans, applications, and AI agents.

🔗 Verifiable Trust specification: https://verana-labs.github.io/verifiable-trust-spec/
