## Overview / About (≤2000 chars)

The Verana Foundation is a nonprofit dedicated to rebuilding digital trust in an era where the web's original promise of openness, privacy, and decentralization has been undermined. In response to the growing challenges of Agentic AI, identity theft, misinformation, and opaque online governance, Verana is stewarding the development of open standards and decentralized infrastructure for secure, verifiable, interoperable, and privacy-respecting communication.

Through its flagship specifications, the Verifiable Trust Specification and the Verifiable Public Registry Specification, the Foundation defines a new trust layer for the internet, built on Verifiable Credentials (VCs), decentralized identifiers (DIDs), and permissioned trust registries.

As the non-profit steward of this open trust layer, the Verana Foundation:

• Owns and hosts the open specifications, authored in the open through public working groups.
• Hosts, stewards, and maintains the open-source reference software (Apache 2.0; copyright held by contributors).
• Issues and administers the VNA utility token, which no party owns.
• Grows the ecosystem through grants, integrations, developer relations, and partnerships.

The Foundation stewards the ecosystem's shared assets, but it does not govern, secure, or operate the live Verana network. That responsibility belongs to the separate and independent Verana Council. This deliberate separation of powers means no single party can capture the trust layer, keeping it open, neutral, and public by design.

Membership is open to organizations and individuals who want to help build a more trustworthy internet.
